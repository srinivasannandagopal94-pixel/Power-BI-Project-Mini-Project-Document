## 📊 Project Overview
●	To analyse income status across a select population based on census data to identify and categorize their income into two i.e., above 50K and less than or equal to 50K.

●	To study various factors and visualize how each factor affect the income of this select population.

●	To determine the most and the least contributing factors.

## 🗂️ Data Source
The dataset was obtained from web scraping. 

**Size:** 2.2 MB 

**Key variables:** Age, Workclass, Education, Marital status, Occupation, Ethnicity, Gender, Working hours, Native country and Income.

- **Visualization:** Power BI

## 💡 Key Insights
The charts were useful in analysing the income status across the select population based on census data to identify and categorize income.

Studied various factors and visualized how each factor affected the income of the population.

The most contributing factors are Marital status, Occupation, Working hours and Education. Married people have higher income than unmarried, separated and divorced individuals. Education and Occupation also play a major role.

The least contributing factors are Native country and Age. There is no considerable difference based on these factors.
